# WORLD BIBLE

## Core Identity

This portfolio is not a traditional website. It is a **cinematic editorial digital experience** set within a designer's office / creative workspace.

The designer is a **UX/UI & Product Designer** who blends AI-driven insight, product thinking, and user-centered design.

---

## World Coherence Rules

### THE ONE SPACE RULE
- **One coherent space only**: Designer's office / creative workspace
- No random environments
- No disconnected scenes
- No location shifts
- The entire experience exists within this singular, cohesive world

### Visual Language
- **Monochrome / Grayscale**: Primary visual palette
- **Premium**: High-end, professional aesthetic
- **Restrained**: Intentional, deliberate design choices
- **Editorial**: Magazine-quality composition and typography
- **Cinematic**: Considered framing, depth, and storytelling
- **Calm and intentional**: Purposeful, never chaotic

### What This Is NOT
- ❌ Cyberpunk overload
- ❌ Gaming aesthetic
- ❌ Neon overload
- ❌ Flashy or distracting effects
- ❌ Multiple random environments
- ❌ Duplicated or repetitive content

### Object Philosophy
- Every object in the workspace must earn its place
- Objects should feel real, grounded, and premium
- No floating elements without purpose
- No holographic gimmicks
- Physical believability over spectacle
- Quality over quantity

### Interaction Principles
- Interactions should feel **intentional**, not accidental
- Motion should serve **narrative**, not decoration
- User agency is respected
- Clarity over cleverness

## Technical Boundaries

### Allowed
- CSS-based animations (when appropriate)
- SVG graphics and illustrations
- Carefully considered JavaScript interactions
- Progressive enhancement
- Responsive design
- Accessibility-first approach

### Requires Approval
- WebGL / Three.js implementation
- 3D rendering
- Complex animations
- Audio/music
- Video elements
- Loading screens

### Forbidden Without Explicit Request
- Auto-playing media
- Intrusive popups
- Cookie banners (unless legally required)
- Tracking scripts
- Third-party analytics (without approval)
