## Scene 2: Opening Desk Scene

### Scene Purpose
Introduce the designer's workspace world. Establish spatial understanding. Show 3 monitors with center monitor as focal portal point. Create calm, premium first impression. Invite exploration.

### Camera Framing
**Wide, slightly elevated perspective**
- Wide shot establishing the desk environment
- Natural viewing angle (as if viewer is standing in the room looking at the desk)
- Slight elevation (eye level or slightly above)
- Horizontal composition emphasizing desk width
- Captures full desk with 3 monitors clearly visible

### Camera Position Logic
Camera is positioned where a visitor would naturally stand when entering the office - a few feet back from the desk, at natural standing eye level. This allows the viewer to see the desk setup as a coherent workspace composition, with clear visibility of all three monitors and the desk surface.

### What Is Visible in Frame
- **Desk surface**: professional, minimal, clean (likely dark wood or matte surface)
- **3 monitors** arranged in believable configuration:
  - Center monitor: larger or positioned as focal point
  - Left and right monitors: flanking, slightly angled inward
  - All monitors powered on with subtle screen glow
- **Minimal desk items**: keyboard, mouse (positioned naturally, not cluttered)
- **Workspace context**: suggestion of walls, ambient lighting, depth
- **Lighting**: desk lamp or ambient lighting creating depth and shadow
- **Surfaces**: desk, possibly shelf edge or wall in background
- Clean, uncluttered composition

### Primary Visual Anchor
**The center monitor** - serves as the portal that will lead into the Identity Hero scene. Visually emphasized through positioning, framing, or subtle lighting.

### Foreground / Midground / Background Structure
**Layered spatial depth**:
- **Foreground**: Front edge of desk, keyboard/mouse
- **Midground**: The 3 monitors (center monitor as primary focus)
- **Background**: Wall, ambient lighting, depth cues suggesting office space
- Atmospheric depth creating sense of real physical space

### Composition Balance
**Centered with lateral symmetry**:
- 3 monitors create symmetrical arrangement (center dominant, flanked by two)
- Desk centered in frame
- Equal negative space on left and right
- Visual weight concentrated in midground (monitors)
- Horizontal composition emphasizing workspace spread
- Generous negative space above and around to create breathing room

### What Must Remain Minimal
- No text or messaging visible yet
- No UI elements or navigation overlays
- No distracting desk clutter (no coffee mugs, papers, random objects yet)
- No busy details competing for attention
- Clean surfaces, intentional object placement
- Restrained environmental details
- Editorial restraint in every element

### What Must NOT Appear Yet
- Identity text or logo on monitor screens (that's Scene 3)
- About Me content
- Project previews
- Recommendations
- Contact information
- Any explicit portfolio content
- User interface elements

### Mood and Atmosphere
**Calm, professional, contemplative**:
- Quiet confidence of a designer's command center
- Premium workspace aesthetic
- Inviting but not aggressive
- Focused and intentional
- Subtle ambient lighting creating depth and warmth
- Sense of entering a real creative professional's office
- Editorial calm - magazine-quality composition

### Monochrome / Premium / Restrained Preservation
- **Monochrome palette**: blacks, grays, whites only (#0a0a0a to #f5f5f5 range)
- **Materials**: dark wood desk, matte monitor housings, metal stand elements
- **Lighting**: soft, diffused, creating tonal depth through grayscale gradations
- **Monitor screens**: subtle glow (soft white/gray luminosity, not bright)
- **Premium materials**: high-quality surfaces, professional equipment
- **Restrained details**: every element intentional, nothing excessive

Camera is positioned as if viewer has just entered and is taking in the workspace. Spatial logic: desk faces into room, monitors visible, walls and depth suggest enclosed office space. This is the spatial anchor for the entire experience.

The following are **explicitly NOT part of this keyframes specification** and must be addressed in the TRANSITIONS_STORYBOARD.md document:

### Camera Movement
- **How** camera moves from scene to scene
- Camera paths and trajectories
- Easing and animation curves
- Movement speed and timing
- Camera rotation or perspective shifts during transitions

### Transition Timing
- Duration of transitions between scenes
- Pacing of camera movements
- Synchronization with user scroll
- Timing of element reveals or hides
- Animation sequencing

### Scroll Choreography
- Scroll distance to scene transition mapping
- Scroll trigger points
- Scroll velocity effects
- User control mechanisms
- Scroll-based animation curves

### Motion Design
- Element animations during transitions
- Fade in/out choreography
- Scale or position animations
- Parallax or depth effects
- Interactive motion responses

### Interaction Patterns
- How users navigate between scenes
- Interactive element behaviors
- Hover states and click interactions
- Project browsing mechanics
- Recommendation viewing interactions
- Contact form/CTA interaction flow

### Loading Transition
- How loading scene transitions to opening desk scene
- Initial reveal choreography
- First camera movement

### Audio Timing
- When/how music begins (if user enables)
- Audio fade in/out points
- Music loop transitions
- Audio-visual synchronization

### Progressive Reveal Logic
- What elements appear first in each scene
- Reveal sequencing for complex scenes
- Loading states for assets
- Progressive enhancement choreography
